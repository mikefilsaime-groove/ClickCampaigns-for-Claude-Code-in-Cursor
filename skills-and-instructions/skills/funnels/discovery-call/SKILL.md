# Discovery Call Funnel

Build high-converting discovery call funnels that transform cold prospects into booked consultations, strategy sessions, or discovery calls while positioning the consultant as the obvious choice.

## Core Objectives

- Maximize consultation bookings and show-up rates
- Qualify leads to ensure only ideal clients book calls
- Build trust and authority before the call
- Reduce no-shows with strategic pre-call nurturing
- Create urgency around booking availability

## Funnel Architecture

### Landing Page / Booking Page

**Above-the-Fold Section:**
- **Headline:** Focus on transformation or outcome achieved
  - "Discover How to [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
  - "Get Your Free [Strategy] Session"
- **Subheadline:** Preview what they'll receive during the call
- **Value Proposition:** What makes this call different and valuable
- **Social Proof:** Logos, testimonials, authority indicators
- **Booking Widget:** Prominent calendar integration (Calendly, Acuity, Cal.com)

**Value Stack Section:**
List 3-5 specific things they'll receive:
- Custom strategy or assessment
- Personalized recommendations
- Next steps roadmap
- Resource guide or toolkit
- Follow-up materials

**Authority & Credibility:**
- Brief bio with credentials and results
- Client success stories or case study highlights
- Testimonials from past call recipients
- [Place Presenter Image or Media Logos Here]

**Urgency & Scarcity:**
- Limited spots available this week/month
- Countdown timer to next available slot
- "Only 3 spots left this week" messaging
- Calendar showing limited availability

**FAQ Section:**
- "What if I'm not ready?"
- "How long is the call?"
- "What happens after?"
- Reassure about no-pressure approach

### Pre-Call Nurture Sequence

**Email 1: Confirmation (Immediate)**
- Thank them for booking
- Confirm date, time, time zone
- Set expectations for the call
- Build anticipation

**Email 2: Pre-Call Questionnaire (2-3 days before)**
- Send intake form or questionnaire
- Explain why answers help serve them better
- Create excitement about personalized approach

**Email 3: Reminder (24 hours before)**
- Friendly reminder with calendar link
- Reiterate value they'll receive
- Address last-minute concerns

**Email 4: Final Reminder (2 hours before)**
- Last-minute reminder
- Zoom/meeting link prominently displayed
- "See you soon!" energy

### Post-Call Follow-Up

**For Completed Calls:**
- Thank you email with next steps
- Send promised resources
- Invite to next step (proposal, onboarding)
- Nurture sequence based on call outcome

**For No-Shows:**
- Empathetic email acknowledging missed call
- Offer to reschedule with urgency
- Provide value even if they don't reschedule
- Win-back sequence with limited-time offer

## Booking Flow Optimization

- **Friction Reduction:** Minimize form fields (name, email, phone max)
- **Time Zone Handling:** Clear display and conversion
- **Buffer Times:** Build in buffer between calls
- **Automated Confirmations:** Instant emails and calendar invites
- **Rescheduling:** Easy option with clear instructions

## Copywriting Style

- Conversational, consultative, authoritative
- Focus on transformation and outcomes
- Empathy-driven language showing understanding
- Create curiosity about what they'll discover
- Build trust through specificity and proof

## Guidelines

**Avoid Booking Page Pitfalls:**
- Never: Make them guess what the call is about
- Never: Use "Book a Call" as your only CTA (too vague)
- Avoid: Requiring too much info upfront (max 3-4 fields)
- Skip: Generic testimonials that don't mention results

**Headline Formulas:**
- "Discover How to [Specific Outcome] Without [Common Pain]"
- "Get Your Free [Type] Strategy Session"
- "[Outcome] in [Timeframe]‚ÄîLet's Map Your Path"
- Bad: "Schedule a Call" / Good: "Get Your Personalized Growth Plan"

**Value Stack Rules:**
- List 3-5 specific things they GET from the call
- Use checkmarks or bullet points
- Each item should be a tangible deliverable
- End with something like "...and more"

**Urgency That Works:**
- Real scarcity: "Only 5 spots available this week"
- Social proof urgency: "Last week, all spots filled in 24 hours"
- Don't fake it: If you're not limited, don't say you are
- Countdown timers work IF the deadline is real

**Pre-Call Sequence Best Practices:**
- Confirmation email within 5 minutes of booking
- Reminder at 24 hours AND 2 hours before
- Include Zoom link in EVERY email
- Make rescheduling easy (reduces no-shows)

**No-Show Reduction Tactics:**
- SMS reminder option (increases show rates by 20-30%)
- Pre-call questionnaire creates commitment
- Send value in advance (they don't want to miss getting more)
- Personal touch: "I'm looking forward to YOUR call specifically"

**Form Field Optimization:**
- Essential: Name, Email, Phone
- Optional: One qualifying question
- Don't ask: Address, company size, budget (too early)
- Every added field reduces conversions by ~10%

**Mobile Considerations:**
- Booking widget must work flawlessly on mobile
- Click-to-call phone number
- Calendar should auto-detect timezone
- Test on iPhone AND Android

## Quality Checklist

- Does the headline clearly communicate booking value?
- Is the booking process frictionless and mobile-friendly?
- Does the pre-call sequence build anticipation?
- Is the value proposition specific and compelling?
- Does this position the consultant as the obvious choice?
- Are objections preemptively addressed?
- Is urgency real and believable?
