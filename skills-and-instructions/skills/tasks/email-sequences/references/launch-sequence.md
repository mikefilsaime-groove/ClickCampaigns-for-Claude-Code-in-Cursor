# Launch Sequence Deep Dive

## The Psychology of Launch Emails

Launch sequences are the highest-converting email type when done correctly. They leverage anticipation, social proof, and urgency in a strategic sequence.

## 7-Email Launch Framework

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Emails 1-2)

#### Email 1: Announcement & Curiosity (3-4 Days Before)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "Something big is coming..."
- "I've been working on something for you"

#### Email 2: Story & Credibility (2 Days Before)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "The story behind [the offer]"
- "Why I created this (and who it's for)"

**Purpose:** Build emotional investment through story

**Structure:**
1. Origin story - why you created this
2. The problem you set out to solve
3. What makes this different
4. Who this is perfect for
5. Reminder of launch date

### Phase 3: Objection Handling (Emails 4-5)

#### Email 4: FAQ & Concerns (Day 2)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "Your questions, answered"
- "Is [Offer] right for you?"
- "Let me address your concerns"

**Purpose:** Preemptively handle objections

**Common Objections to Address:**
1. **Price:** "I can't afford it"
2. **Time:** "I don't have time"
3. **Skepticism:** "I've tried things before"
4. **Fit:** "Will this work for my situation?"
5. **Timing:** "Is now the right time?"

**Structure:**
```
Opening:
"I've been getting questions about [Offer Name].

Let me address the most common ones:"

FAQ Format:
"Q: What if I don't have time for this?
A: [Specific answer showing how it works for busy people]

#### Email 5: Social Proof Deep Dive (Day 3)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "See what [customer] achieved"
- "Real results from real people"
- "[Specific result] in [timeframe]"

**Purpose:** Build belief through proof

**Structure:**
- 2-3 detailed testimonials with specific results
- Before/after story
- Common patterns in success stories
- How they can achieve similar results

### Phase 4: Urgency (Emails 6-7)

#### Email 6: Urgency Introduction (Day 4-5)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "48 hours left..."
- "The clock is ticking"
- "[Scarcity element] ending soon"

**Purpose:** Create genuine urgency

**Structure:**
```
Opening:
"Quick reminder: [scarcity element] ends in 48 hours."

Reminder of Value:
"When you join [Offer Name], you get:
[Quick value recap]"

CTA:

#### Email 7: Final Hours (Last Day)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "FINAL HOURS: [Offer Name]"
- "Tonight at midnight, this ends"
- "Last chance, {first_name}"

**Purpose:** Maximum urgency for final conversions

**Structure:**
- Clear deadline
- Quick value recap
- Final testimonial
- Guarantee reminder
- Multiple CTAs
- Emotional close

### Phase 5: Close (Email 8 - Optional)

#### Email 8: Final Call (Last Hours)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "2 hours remaining"
- "I didn't want you to miss this"
- "Before I close the doors..."

**Purpose:** Last emotional appeal

**Structure:**
- Personal note
- What they'll miss
- Final guarantee reminder
- Deadline
- Genuine close

## Launch Sequence Best Practices

### Timing
- Email 1: 3-4 days before launch
- Email 2: 1-2 days before launch
- Email 3: Launch day, morning
- Email 4: Day 2
- Email 5: Day 3
- Email 6: Day 4-5
- Email 7: Final day, morning
- Email 8: Final day, evening (hours before close)

### Subject Line Testing
- Test subject lines on email 3 (highest stakes)
- Personalization ({first_name}) typically performs better
- Emoji use depends on audience

### Urgency Ethics
- Only use real scarcity
- Real deadlines
- Real limited spots
- Real price increases
- Never fake urgency‚Äîit destroys trust
